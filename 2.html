Competition Rules
  • Chess Clock:
    Each player must make their moves within a specified time limit, controlled by a chess clock.

  • Recording Moves:
    Players must record their moves on a scoresheet to keep track of the game.
  • Drawn Game: The game can end in a draw under several conditions, such as stalemate, threefold repetition, or insufficient material to checkmate.
  • Conduct of Players: Players must behave in a sportsmanlike manner and follow the instructions of the arbiter.

Additional Regulations:
  • Anti-Cheating Measures: Organizers must implement anti-cheating measures, such as electronic device checks and surveillance.
  • Dress Code: Players are expected to adhere to a dress code specified by the organizers.
  • Arbiters: The role of the arbiter is to ensure fair play and resolve disputes during the game.

Candidate Belongings
    Candidates participating in the tournament are expected to adhere to the following guidelines regarding their belongings:
  • Electronic Devices: Players are not allowed to bring electronic devices, such as mobile phones, tablets, or smartwatches, into the playing area. These devices must be left in a designated area or with the organizers.
  • Personal Items: Players are allowed to bring personal items, such as water bottles, snacks, and writing materials, into the playing area. However, these items must be kept in a manner that does not disturb other players.
  • Identification: Players must carry a valid identification document, such as a passport or national ID card, for verification purposes.
  • Chess Equipment: Players are encouraged to bring their own chess sets and clocks, although the organizers will provide these items if necessary.
